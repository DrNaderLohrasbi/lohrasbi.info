Blueprint 15 · Semantic Interoperability

Medical NLP Interoperability Pipeline

A universal semantic translation layer that converts clinical text into standardized, computable, multi-language medical data — dissolving documentation silos and forming the national semantic backbone for analytics and AI reasoning.

Extended Architecture Overview

The Medical NLP Interoperability Pipeline unifies clinical documentation across languages, institutions, EHR vendors, and legacy systems. It transforms unstructured narratives into standardized representations such as FHIR, SNOMED CT, ICD-10, and custom national schemas — enabling cross-system reasoning, analytics, and decision support at scale.

Core Components

  • Multi-language medical NLP engine
  • Structured output layer (FHIR, SNOMED, ontology graphs)
  • Semantic normalization algorithms
  • Cross-institution interoperability interface
  • National documentation intelligence registry

Dependencies

Upstream: Statewide NLP inputs, physician documentation, diagnostic narratives
Downstream: Reasoning engines, continuity-of-care systems, national analytics
