Blueprint 03 · Crisis Architecture

Crisis Architecture Framework

A national crisis-operating architecture enabling real-time sensing, prediction, prioritization, and coordinated response across hospitals, emergency systems, and population-level signals.

Extended Architecture Overview

The Crisis Architecture Framework defines the structural, algorithmic, and operational components required for a nation to function coherently during emergencies. It integrates clinical, operational, environmental, and geopolitical signals into one unified system capable of anticipating crises before they escalate.

Core Crisis Components

  • National early-warning and risk-sensing grid
  • Real-time surge prediction and capacity orchestration
  • Cross-agency emergency coordination logic
  • Crisis-mode workflow overrides for hospitals and EMS
  • Integration with Health Nation OS for sovereign control

Dependencies

Upstream: Architecture Over Repair, National Strategy
Downstream: Crisis Operations Center, Predictive Emergency Mobility Network
