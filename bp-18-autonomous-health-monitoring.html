Blueprint 18 · Autonomous Monitoring Layer

Autonomous Health Monitoring

A continuous, autonomous sensing architecture that detects physiological instability, behavioral changes, environmental exposures, and early signals of deterioration long before clinical escalation occurs.

Extended Architecture Overview

The Autonomous Health Monitoring Layer establishes a national always-on sensing grid that integrates clinical biomarkers, wearable streams, ambient data, behavioral indicators, and risk signatures. It continuously evaluates patient trajectories and predicts when intervention is required.

Core Components

  • Continuous physiologic data ingestion
  • Deterioration prediction models
  • Behavioral and environmental signal fusion
  • Real-time alerting and escalation pathways
  • Integration with national control loops

Dependencies

Upstream: Digital Phenotyping Engines, Patient Journey Models
Downstream: Crisis Activation Systems, Hospital Autopilot Modules
